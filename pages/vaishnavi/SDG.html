Business case overview

The finalized business case with which I will be working in this course project is:

  • Traffic flow and logistic management within SEZ
      loading/unloading schedules

Issues with Loading/Unloading Schedules:

  • When there are long delays between scheduled loading or unloading times, trucks and workers are idle. This happens when:
    • Loading/unloading times are not synchronized with truck arrivals.
    • There is a mismatch in the time window allotted for each activity.
    • Delays in one task push back other tasks.
  • Multiple trucks are scheduled to load or unload at the same time at the same location (e.g., docks or warehouses). This can create congestion and result in:
    • Trucks being delayed because they cannot access the dock or loading area.
  • Poor estimation of the time required for loading/unloading activities.

Effects:

  • Idle trucks, labor, and equipment increase overall costs for fuel, manpower, and maintenance.
  • Delays require rescheduling, which adds to the costs of managing logistics and transportation.
  • Delays in loading/unloading can affect delivery timelines, especially for time-sensitive goods.
  • When tasks are delayed or rescheduled, the whole supply chain can be impacted.
  • Trucks and equipment are often underused if schedules are not optimized. This results in the need for more assets to handle the same volume of goods.
