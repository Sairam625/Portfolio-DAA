Smart Parking System

The Smart Parking System aims to efficiently manage parking spaces in crowded urban areas, addressing challenges and promoting sustainable practices in alignment with SDG targets.

Challenges Faced

  • Allocating available parking spaces dynamically.
  • Reducing time spent searching for parking.
  • Implementing differential pricing based on demand.

SDG Targets Addressed

  • SDG 11.2: By 2030, provide access to safe, affordable, accessible and sustainable transport systems for all, improving road safety, notably by expanding public transport, with special attention to the needs of those in vulnerable situations, women, children, persons with disabilities and older persons.
  • Reason: By reducing parking search times, it promotes safe and affordable transportation.

Algorithms Used

  1. Binary Search: The system uses a sorted list of available spaces for quick allocation.
  2. Heap: A min-heap tracks the smallest available parking space dynamically.
  3. Sliding Window: Monitors occupancy trends over a fixed time period.
  4. Differential Pricing: Parking fees are calculated based on the duration of stay, encouraging quick turnover during high demand.

Space and Time Efficiency

    1. Binary Search (Using `set` for Sorted Parking Spaces)

    Time Complexity: O(log n) for insertion and removal of elements.

    Space Complexity: O(n) for storing the parking spaces.

    2. Sliding Window (Tracking Parking History)

    Time Complexity: O(n) for checking and removing cars that have been parked for too long.

    Space Complexity: O(n) for storing parking history.

    3. Heap (Min-Heap for Space Allocation)

    Time Complexity: O(log n) for inserting and removing spaces.

    Space Complexity: O(n) for maintaining the heap structure.

    4. Differential Pricing (Calculating Parking Fees)

    Time Complexity: O(1) for calculating the fee for each car.

    Space Complexity: O(1) for storing and calculating fees.

Overall Efficiency

  • Time Complexity: O(m log n) for m operations and n parking spaces.
  • Space Complexity: O(n + m) for available and occupied spaces.
