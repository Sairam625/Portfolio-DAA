Smart Parking System

The Smart Parking System aims to efficiently manage parking spaces in crowded urban areas, addressing challenges and promoting sustainable practices in alignment with SDG targets.

Challenges Faced

  • Allocating available parking spaces dynamically.
  • Reducing time spent searching for parking.
  • Implementing differential pricing based on demand.

SDG Targets Addressed

  • SDG 11.2: By reducing parking search times, it promotes safe and affordable transportation.
  • SDG 13.2: Encourages efficient land use and reduces emissions by minimizing vehicle idling.

Algorithms Used

  1. Initialization: The parking lot is initialized with a specified number of spaces.
  2. Binary Search: The system uses a sorted list of available spaces for quick allocation.
  3. Heap: A min-heap tracks the smallest available parking space dynamically.
  4. Sliding Window: Monitors occupancy trends over a fixed time period.
  5. Differential Pricing: Parking fees are calculated based on the duration of stay, encouraging quick turnover during high demand.
