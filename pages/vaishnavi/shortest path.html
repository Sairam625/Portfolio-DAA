Shortest Path

We manage an automated logistics scheduling system for a large Distribution Centre (DC) handling trucks for multiple warehouses, docks, and storage facilities.

Business Case Relating to SDG

Target 12.3: Halve per capita global food waste by reducing losses in production and supply chains
  • Indicator 12.3.1: Food loss index.
  • Relation: Improved scheduling reduces downtime for perishable goods, minimizing losses during transit within SEZs.
Target 11.2: Provide access to safe, affordable, sustainable transport systems for all
  • Indicator 11.2.1: Proportion of the population that has convenient access to public transport.
  • Relation: Improved traffic flow in SEZs minimizes congestion, creating spillover effects of better accessibility and reducing delays for transport systems linked to the SEZ.

Real-Time Scenario

  • Distribution Centre (DC): The main facility where goods are loaded/unloaded.
  • Warehouses (W1, W2, W3): Storage facilities for goods pick-up or drop-off.
  • Docks (D1, D2, D3): Locations for unloading containers from trucks.
  • Routes: Represented by weighted edges indicating time or distance in minutes.

The trucks need to go through multiple locations to either load or unload goods. We will use Dijkstra's algorithm to determine the shortest path for the trucks and assign the tasks accordingly.

Node Mapping

  • 0: DC (Distribution Centre)
  • 1: W1 (Warehouse 1)
  • 2: W2 (Warehouse 2)
  • 3: W3 (Warehouse 3)
  • 4: D1 (Delivery point 1)

Efficiency

  • Time Efficiency: O(V^2), where V is the number of vertices, since both finding the smallest distance and updating neighbors dominate.
  • Space Efficiency: O(V^2), due to the adjacency matrix.
