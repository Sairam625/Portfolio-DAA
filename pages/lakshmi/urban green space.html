Urban Green Space Management for Sustainable Cities

About SDG 11

Urban Green Space Management directly supports SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), which aims to make cities inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. By prioritizing green spaces, we enhance biodiversity, reduce pollution, and provide recreational zones for urban populations. Effective green space management ensures accessibility, boosts community well-being, and mitigates the urban heat island effect.
SDG 11.7.1: "Average share of the built-up area of cities that is open space for public use for all, by sex and age group."

Business Case Overview

Urban green spaces, such as parks and recreational zones, are essential for enhancing the quality of life, improving ecological balance, and maintaining biodiversity in cities. Managing these spaces effectively with algorithms helps optimize their layout, usage, and maintenance.

Algorithms for Green Space Management

  • Binary Search Tree (BST): Efficient for managing and searching green space locations.
  • Kruskal's Algorithm: Optimizes the connectivity of green spaces with minimal resource usage.
  • Segment Tree: Handles queries and updates on green space areas efficiently.
  • Heap: Prioritizes green spaces by usage frequency or ecological importance.

Time and Space Efficiency

  • Binary Search Tree (BST):

    Time Complexity: \( O(\log n) \) average, \( O(n) \) worst-case for insertion, deletion, and search.

    Space Complexity: \( O(n) \), where \( n \) is the number of nodes.

  • Kruskal's Algorithm:

    Time Complexity: \( O(E \log E + V) \), where \( E \) is the number of edges and \( V \) is the number of vertices.

    Space Complexity: \( O(E + V) \) for storing graph data and results.

  • Segment Tree:

    Time Complexity: \( O(\log n) \) for updates and queries.

    Space Complexity: \( O(4n) \) for tree storage, where \( n \) is the number of elements.

  • Heap:

    Time Complexity: \( O(\log n) \) for insertion and deletion.

    Space Complexity: \( O(n) \), where \( n \) is the number of elements.
