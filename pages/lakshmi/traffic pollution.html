Vehicle Restriction for Pollution Reduction

Business Case

SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities) emphasizes creating cities that are inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. Managing vehicle emissions is vital for reducing air pollution, improving health, and fostering a cleaner urban environment.

Proposed Solutions Using Algorithms

  • Queue and Stack:

    Queue: Model vehicles at traffic signals for efficient flow management, minimizing delays and emissions.

    Stack: Alternate-day vehicle access management using a stack (e.g., even/odd-numbered vehicles).

  • Dijkstra’s Algorithm:

    Finds the shortest path for vehicles while factoring in restrictions and pollution zones.

  • Heap:

    Prioritizes vehicles based on emission levels for optimized restriction strategies.

  • Binary Search Tree (BST):

    Tracks vehicle registrations efficiently based on emission levels for quick lookup.

  • AVL Tree:

    Maintains a dynamic, balanced record of vehicles, adjusting to real-time changes in restrictions.

Efficiency of Algorithms

  • Queue and Stack:

    Time Complexity: \( O(1) \) for enqueue, dequeue, push, and pop.

    Space Complexity: \( O(n) \) for managing \( n \) vehicles.

  • Dijkstra’s Algorithm:

    Time Complexity: \( O(E + V \log V) \), where \( V \) is vertices, and \( E \) is edges.

    Space Complexity: \( O(V + E) \).

  • Heap:

    Time Complexity: \( O(\log n) \) for insertions and deletions.

    Space Complexity: \( O(n) \) for storing \( n \) elements.

  • Binary Search Tree (BST):

    Time Complexity: \( O(\log n) \) average; \( O(n) \) worst case.

    Space Complexity: \( O(n) \).

  • AVL Tree:

    Time Complexity: \( O(\log n) \) for search, insertion, and deletion.

    Space Complexity: \( O(n) \).

Input Example

Vehicle data with different emission levels:

    Vehicle ABC123, Emission Level: 100
    Vehicle XYZ456, Emission Level: 150
    Vehicle LMN789, Emission Level: 200

Output Example

Restricted vehicles based on emissions:

    Restricted Vehicle: XYZ456, Emission Level: 150
    Restricted Vehicle: LMN789, Emission Level: 200
