Urban Noise Pollution Monitoring and Mitigation

About SDG 11

Sustainable Development Goal 11 (SDG 11) focuses on creating cities and communities that are inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. Noise pollution negatively affects urban health and quality of life. By leveraging algorithms for monitoring and mitigation, this business case contributes to the betterment of urban environments, aligning with SDG 11 to promote well-being and ecological balance.

Solution Overview Using Algorithms

The following algorithms are applied to monitor and mitigate urban noise pollution effectively:

  • Brute Force String Search: Detects specific noise patterns in recorded sound data.
  • Rabin Karp Algorithm: Efficiently identifies patterns in noise data logs for specific sources.
  • Skip List: Organizes and manages noise data across city zones for quick access and updates.
  • Lookup Table: Maps noise levels to predefined mitigation strategies for actionable insights.

Input for the Code

The system takes the following noise data as input:

    Noise Data: "trafficconstructiontraffictraffic"
    Noise Pattern: "construction"
    Noise Levels (in decibels): 100, 75, 80, 90

Output for the Code

The algorithms process the input and produce the following results:

    Brute Force Search:
    Pattern "construction" found in the noise data.

    Rabin-Karp Algorithm:
    Pattern "construction" found in the noise data.

    Skip List:
    Noise levels managed in the skip list: 75, 80, 90, 100.

    Lookup Table:
    Mitigation Strategy for High Noise Level: "Implement noise barriers and restrict construction during peak hours."

Time and Space Efficiency

  • Brute Force String Search:

    Time Complexity: \( O(m \cdot n) \), where \( m \) is the pattern length and \( n \) is the text length.

    Space Complexity: \( O(1) \), as it requires no additional data structures.

  • Rabin Karp Algorithm:

    Time Complexity: \( O(n + m) \) average, \( O(n \cdot m) \) worst-case with hash collisions.

    Space Complexity: \( O(1) \), as it uses rolling hash calculations.

  • Skip List:

    Time Complexity: \( O(\log n) \) for insertion, deletion, and search.

    Space Complexity: \( O(n) \), where \( n \) is the number of nodes.

  • Lookup Table:

    Time Complexity: \( O(1) \) for lookups.

    Space Complexity: \( O(k) \), where \( k \) is the number of noise levels mapped to strategies.
