1.Public Transport Optimization

Problem:

Anandnagari's public transport system faces inefficiencies in its routing, leading to:

  • Underutilization of buses.
  • Increased commute times for residents.
  • Higher operational costs due to redundant or inefficient routes.
  • Elevated carbon emissions due to unnecessary fuel consumption.

Solution:

The Floyd-Warshall Algorithm can be used to calculate the shortest paths between all pairs of bus stops. This information helps to design optimized routes that:

  • Reduce travel times for passengers.
  • Enhance the efficiency of public transport services.
  • Minimize the operational costs and environmental footprint.

Alignment with SDG Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

Target 11.2: By 2030, provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems for all, improving road safety, notably by expanding public transport.

Indicator 11.2.1: Proportion of population that has convenient access to public transport, by sex, age, and persons with disabilities.

How It Aligns

  • Affordable Transport: Optimized routes ensure reduced fuel consumption and operational costs, allowing for lower fare structures, making public transport more affordable.
  • Accessibility: Ensures all neighborhoods, including underserved ones, are efficiently connected, enhancing inclusivity.
  • Environmental Sustainability: Reduction in redundant routes leads to decreased carbon emissions, contributing to cleaner air quality.
  • Resilient Infrastructure: By creating efficient and adaptable route systems, the city ensures resilience in the face of urbanization and population growth.
  • Improved Mobility: Faster and more reliable public transport encourages usage, reducing dependence on private vehicles and mitigating traffic congestion.

efficiency analysis of dijkstra's

    • Binary Min-Heap:
      • Time Complexity: O((V + E) log V)
      • When to Use: Sparse graphs (common case).
    • Array as Priority Queue:
      • Time Complexity: O(V2)
      • When to Use: Dense graphs or small graphs.
    • Fibonacci Heap:
      • Time Complexity: O(V log V + E)
      • When to Use: Theoretically optimal, but rarely used.

efficiency analysis of Floyd-Warshall

  • Time Complexity:
    • Overall: O(V3)
    • Due to three nested loops iterating over all vertices.
  • Space Complexity:
    • Overall: O(V2)
    • Uses a distance matrix to store shortest path information.
  • Advantages:
    • Handles negative edge weights.
    • Simple and easy to implement.
    • Useful for dense graphs.
  • Disadvantages:
    • Not efficient for sparse graphs.
    • Fails in the presence of negative weight cycles.

  • Environmental Benefits: Less congestion means reduced fuel use and fewer greenhouse gas emissions, aligning with climate action goals (SDG Goal 13).

3.Emergency Evacuation Route Optimization

Problem:

During emergencies like floods or fires, Anandnagary faces challenges such as:

  • Inefficient evacuation routes leading to delays.
  • Confusion among residents due to a lack of clear paths.
  • Increased risk of injuries and loss of life.
  • Difficulty in coordinating evacuation efforts.

Solution:

  • Swift evacuation during emergencies.
  • Safe and well-coordinated movement of people.
  • Minimal confusion and panic among residents.

Alignment with SDG Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

Target 11.5: By 2030, significantly reduce the number of deaths and the number of people affected by disasters, including through disaster risk reduction strategies.

Indicator 11.5.1: Number of deaths, missing persons, and persons affected by disaster per 100,000 people.

How It Aligns

  • Safe and Swift Evacuation: Identifies the shortest and safest paths to minimize casualties and ensure timely evacuation.
  • Disaster Resilience: Enhances the city’s preparedness and response during emergencies.
  • Inclusive Safety: Ensures evacuation plans cover all areas, including underserved neighborhoods.
  • Infrastructure Planning: Helps design effective emergency routes and improves city resilience.
  • Risk Reduction: Contributes to reducing disaster-related injuries and fatalities, aligning with climate action goals (SDG Goal 13).

Relating this business case to algortims

    The Bread First Search (BFS) algorithm can be applied to identify the quickest evacuation routes from any location in the city.

Breadth-First Search (BFS) Efficiency Analysis

  • Time Complexity:
    • Overall: O(V + E)
    • Where V is the number of vertices and E is the number of edges.
    • Each vertex is visited once, and each edge is explored once in the adjacency list representation.
  • Space Complexity:
    • Overall: O(V + E)
    • Space required for adjacency list, visited array, and queue.

4.Smart Water Management System

Objective

Ensure sustainable water usage and prevent wastage.

Key Features

  • Smart Meters: Monitor water usage and detect leaks in real-time.
  • Rainwater Harvesting Systems: Collect and store rainwater for non-potable uses.
  • Recycling Plants: Treat and reuse wastewater for irrigation and industrial processes.

Benefits

  • Conservation of precious water resources.
  • Cost savings for households and industries.
  • Improved resilience to water scarcity.

Alignment with SDG Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

This business case aligns with SDG Goal 11 by contributing to sustainable urban development through efficient water management. Specifically, it addresses the following targets under Goal 11:

  • Target 11.6: Reduce the environmental impact of cities by improving water resource management.

    • Indicator 11.6.1: Proportion of urban solid waste regularly collected and with adequate final discharge.

  • Target 11.b: Promote sustainable practices and resilience to climate-related water challenges.

    • Indicator 11.b.1: Proportion of local governments that adopt and implement local disaster risk reduction strategies in line with the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015–2030.

  • Target 11.c: Support sustainable and resilient urban infrastructure development.

    • Indicator 11.c.1: Proportion of financial support to the development of sustainable and resilient infrastructure in developing countries.

Relating this business case to algortims

    The Kruskals algorithm can be used in this business case to design an optimal water distribution network by minimizing the cost of connecting all reservoirs, treatment plants, and user locations. Its edge-based approach ensures the network is built efficiently without redundancy, making it ideal for infrastructure planning.
